      Close Little Stepping Stones.

      Four touchpoints across different channels. Most prospects respond by Touch 2 or 3. These scripts are your template for every future prospect — swap the highlighted fields for each new target.

      1
      Contact FormDay 1

      Website Contact Form — Spark curiosity, share the link

      Submit through their site. Goal: get her to click the link. One idea, one link, done.

      Hi [Noelle] — I am a local web designer based in [Wake Forest] and I came across Little Stepping Stones while looking into childcare in Garner. I was so impressed by your story — 20 years, multiple Best Daycare awards, the community you have built — that I put together a concept for what your website could look like.

      No pitch, no pressure. I just built it and thought you should see it: [your-proposal-link]

      Happy to chat if anything resonates. — [Your Name], White Haus Media

      Keep under 500 characters. Do not mention pricing. The link does the selling.

      2
      Facebook / LinkedIn DMDay 2

      Direct Message to Noelle — One sentence, same link

      Find Noelle via the Little Stepping Stones Facebook page or LinkedIn. Not re-pitching — just making sure she saw it.

      Hi Noelle — I sent something through your website yesterday that I think you will genuinely enjoy seeing. It is a working concept for a new Little Stepping Stones website built around your story. Here is the link: [your-proposal-link]

      Facebook: DM the Little Stepping Stones page directly addressed to Noelle. LinkedIn: search "Noelle Pittman Little Stepping Stones Garner."

      3
      Phone CallDay 4

      Call (919) 550-0076 — Ask for Noelle by name

      60 seconds max. Confirming the link landed and getting her direct email.

      When answered: "Hi, could I speak with Noelle please?"

      To Noelle: "Hi Noelle, my name is [Your Name] from White Haus Media. I reached out through your website this week. I built a free concept for a new Little Stepping Stones website — did the link come through?"

      If yes: "What did you think? Happy to walk you through it at your convenience."

      If no: "No problem — could I send it to your direct email so it does not get lost?"

      Goal: confirm she saw it. Secondary goal: get her direct email. Do not discuss pricing unless she asks.

      4
      Walk InDay 7

      10955 Cleveland Road — Show up, be human

      Tue/Wed/Thu, 9:30–11am or 1–3pm. Proposal open on phone. Printed one-pager in hand.

      At front desk: "Hi, I am [Your Name] — a local web designer. I have been trying to reach Noelle about a project I put together for Little Stepping Stones. Is she available for two minutes?"

      To Noelle: "Hi Noelle — I am [Your Name] from White Haus Media. I built a working version of what a new Little Stepping Stones website could look like — free, no obligation. Can I show you on my phone real quick?"

      Leaving: Hand one-pager. "All details including pricing are here. No rush — I am local, just call or text whenever."

      Bring: phone with proposal open, printed one-pager, business card. Best days: Tue/Wed/Thu. Avoid Monday mornings and Friday afternoons.
